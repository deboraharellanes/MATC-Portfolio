Context,  Audience, and Purpose
Sometime ago, I don’t know when, my dad bought a 1970 Beetle. It changed hands a few times, till it was passed down to me. By then it needed a lot of work. I found a small VW repair shop—and little did I know, I was walking into the hub of a community of Vintage VW connoisseurs and collectors. I became friends with the owners and within a few years found myself immersed.

My idea for the website started as a tribute to all the great people that keep old VW’s running. It evolved into a beginner’s repair guide. My audience is mechanics who know already know how to work on cars but need specifics about VW’s. Or beginning mechanics needing to learn the basics.

Design Process
I collaborated with a VW mechanic who works out of his garage. All the car parts in the photos belong to him, and the demonstration photos are him removing an engine, then disassembling it, and then installing. He explained to me how a 4-stroke engine works, as well as the other processes.   It is all white background with a contrasting black menu bar with black text and graphics. I experimented with text colors but didn’t like how any other turned out. I used Helvetica on all the text to keep it straightforward and readable. I used Architects Daughter typeface in the navigation bar, to give it some variation and style. I used a combination of real-life photos and illustrated graphics

Strengths
A user can easily find information and it has an abundance of relevent graphics. The naviagtion bar is in large Helvetica print for easy use and readability. I coded the website in HTML, instead of using preformatted drag and drop software. It takes longer this way, but gives the author a lot more freedom with design choices and interactive functions. I am taking classes to learn them better.

Weakness
My VW website is a 1990's level of sophistication. Eventhough I incorporated user experience and design concepts to the best of my ability, it still doesn't have any interactive capabilities like contact me (email), submt (forms and applications), or online purchases. In order for me include these advanced functions, I still need to learn more about web development. Web page interactve capabilities are developed through programing languages called Javascript and PHP, which I am in the process of learning. My plan is to modernize it after I learn more.

Final Thoughts
I first learned website design in the 90's, but didn't persue it. Since then, the industry has made huge advancements. This was my first step in relearning. I learn by doing. Hope my momentum continues and I can learn more.
