Context, Audience, and Purpose
My purpose for designing a solar energy infographic is to to raise awareness and I designed it to be easily skimmed. My audience is those who have heard about renewable energy but havenâ€™t invested any time in learning about it. It is introductory, providing a general explanation of a hugely complex issue. After decades of research, solar energy is only starting to gain popularity and there is no shortage of barriers.

Design Process
From beginning to end, I mapped out each element using contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity. I contrasted brown against a white background and repeated that color scheme in center alignment throughout. White text in the title bar contrasts against the brown background, with the sun graphic beginning the center alignment. Under that, a horizontal brown bar creates reptition and a centered globe reinforces the alignment. Directly under that I centered a recyling icon, and then a brown horizontal bar for repetition, and then a center-aligned solar panel graphic.

The words Pros and Cons repeat color and font style, and are backed against the left and right margins to reinforce center alignment.Transitioning to the list, brown slabs are repeated, contrasting against the white text. In close proximity, the black text contrasts against the white background. The brown center-aligned vertical margin contrasts against the white backdrop and continues repetition.

I learned that multi-colored graphics can be more effective when their design scheme contrasts well with the background. So I placed the white top-right to meet the bottom of the brown center-aligned bar, above the footer. This continues center alignment, contrast, and repetition. The footer is a slab of brown with contrasting white text. The brown overlay creates proximity and alignment with color and graphics. The chunk of white space at the top and then all the text placed in the bottom three/fourths creates proximity.

Strengths
Effective design elements make the infographic vibrant. Large print ensures ease of reading. The information is relevant, up-to-date, and important to understand. I included every pro and con that I could find out and a blurb that is as acurate and informative as possible. A reader with a vague prior background can easily digest the overall message.

Weaknesses
I created the infographic using VISME Design software, which has templates, fonts, and graphics to design almost any kind of media. When I clicked infographic I was routed to a plethora of templates and an option for a blank page. The only size option for an infographic is long and narrow and horizontal information did not fit well. The small print was unreadable, and decorative fonts were blurry, so I had to use a Visme font called Lato, a Helvetica equivalent, which I would not have if I had other options. Such large print on the narrow page gives it a cluttered look.

The pros and cons list is abridged, only hitting the high points of enormous complexities. Each pro and con already has numerous books written, and many are outdated because of rapid technological advancement.

Final Thoughts
VISME design software provided loads of templates that I experimented with to help me decide on the most professional look. I tried out different colors, texts, graphics, and alignments. I thought through what kind of information is really important and which placement will be most effective.
