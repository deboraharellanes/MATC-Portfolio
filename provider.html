Context, Audience and Purpose
Provider Farms sell raw goat and cow milk, grass-fed meat, and other farm products that they make. They are based in Mountain Home. They fill their van with their products and drive to their pick-up sites, which are parking lots of businesses. They have a website for information and ordering, and business cards, but other than that they find customers through word of mouth. They need a brochure to hand out or hang on bulletin boards. My purpose is to attract customers and interest them in wanting to buy the products. Their prices are higher than the local grocery stores. My audience is quite open; any person willing to read the brochure is a potential customer. Although, the majority of people who would be willing to go out of thier way to buy farm fresh products would tend be those interested in health and nutrition.

Design Process
I created this version of the brochure with Visme. The brick red color and the borders alignment repeat through the brochure and contrast against the white background and black text. The different groups of information, like contact, about the cows, the glass jar exchange, and pick-up information are placed, topic-by-topic all with relevent proximity. I used two images redolent of farm life, an old-fashioned milk & cream image (late 1800s) and a young girl milking a cow. All the text is sans serif and in left alignment, with the exception of the title Raw Cow and Goat Milk which is centered.

Strengths
The brick red color of the border contrast well against the white background, the Provider Farms logo, and the old style milk graphic. The over-all look is warm and friendly and easily digestible. The information is up-to-date, and the brochure is organized and uncluttered. I used large print san serif for readability. The brochure includes all of the necessary information for a customer to get started right away ordering and picking-up products.

Weakness
The first time I designed this tri-fold I used a InDesign, which is a paid subscription. Also InDesign only gave me the option to save as a PDF. Not a jpg. Because of these drawbacks, I recreated it using Visme. With Visme, I resized their family photo, making it smaller and it went blurry. So I couldn't use it. I googled a farm images and found a suitable one. Also Visme's text boxes only handle smaller amounts of text. Also Visme color palettes seem less vibrant. One icon I downloaded, I couldn't match it up with the colors from Visme. I didn't like the font options but I used them because they sans serif and increase readable. It is a helvetica equivalent. I used it because it would be more clear. I wanted a typeface more suggestive of a old-style writing, to accent their life-style. With all those negatives, I was able to nicely reproduce their brochure. Also this brochure is a trifold and would not work as on on-line document. The design is meant to be folded.

Final Thoughts
I was able to develop collaborative skills by interacting with clients and learning their story. I experimented with InDesign and Visme and integrated the design principles and rhetoric.
